Michael Schumacher

The most successful racer in Formula 1 history

Michael Schumacher, celebrating his 52nd victory at Belgium in 2001,where he surpassed Alain Prost for the record of most wins by anyFormula 1 driver in history.

    Michael Schumacher is arguably one of the greatest racers in the history of Formula 1 racing. Throughout his career, he achieved over 300 grand prix starts, 91 wins, 7 world championships, and a reputation as a fierce and determined racer.

  • 1969: Born on January 3 in Huerth-Hermuelheim, Germany.
  • 1973: Makes his racing debut in a kart race at the age of four
  • 1987: Wins German and European kart championships.
  • 1988: Finishes fourth in German Formula Ford championship and second in the European series in first year of car racing.
  • 1990: Wins first major single-seater title, clinching the German Formula Three crown. Steps up to sports cars, driving for Mercedes
  • Debut with Jordan (1991): Michael Schumacher made his Formula 1 debut at the 1991 Belgian Grand Prix, driving for the Jordan-Ford team as a stand-in for Bertrand Gachot. Despite retiring on the first lap of the race due to a clutch problem, Schumacher's performance during the weekend left a lasting impression..
    • At the Spa-Francorchamps circuit in Belgium, Michael Schumacher drove the Jordan 191, qualifying 7th for his first grand prix but retiring from the race early.
  • Benneton-Ford (1991-1995): Schumacher's impressive debut earned him a seat with the Benneton-Ford team for the remainder of the 1991 season. He continued with Benneton for the next four years, achieving significant milestones. In 1992, he achieved his first podium in Mexico and his first Grand Prix victory in Belgium. He finished third in the championship that year.
  • Move to Ferrari (1996): In 1996, Schumacher embarked on a historic move to Ferrari, a team that had struggled for years to secure World Championships. The decision to join Ferrari proved instrumental in shaping both his career and the team's fortunes.
  • First World Championships (1994 and 1995): Schumacher secured his first two World Championships, first with Benetton-Ford in 1994 and then with Ferrari in 1995. His achievements made him the youngest double world champion at the time.
  • Controversy in 1994: The 1994 season was marred by controversy, including Schumacher's disqualification and a two-race ban. However, the most notable incident was the collision with Damon Hill in the final race. Schumacher's crash secured his first world championship, although it was later revealed that the collision was intentional.
    • A stern Schumacher after his brawl with Coulthard and McLaren staff at the 1998 Belgian GP.
  • Dominance with Ferrari (2000-2004): Schumacher's era with Ferrarinis considered one of the most dominant periods in Formula 1 history. He won five consecutive World Championships from 2000 to 2004,setting numerous records and achieving a remarkable 48 race wins during this period. His success played a pivotal role in transforming Ferrari into a championship-winning team
  • Return with Mercedes (2010-2012): After a brief retirement, Schumacher returned to Formula 1 with the Mercedes team in 2010. Although he couldn't replicate the same level of success as before, his comeback was a significant moment for the sport.
  • Second Retirement (2012): Schumacher retired for the second time at the end of the 2012 season when Mercedes signed Lewis Hamilton. His final race took place at the 2012 Brazilian Grand Prix.
  • 2013: December 29 - Michael Schumacher is in a critical condition in hospital after a skiing accident in the French Alps.
  • 2014: June 16 - Schumacher had regained consciousness and left Grenoble Hospital for further rehabilitation at his home in Switzerland.
  • Ongoing Legacy: Fans around the world have expressed their ongoing admiration and respect for Michael. The news of his accident and the following lack of progress in his recovery has hit the motorsport community hard. Motorsport is dangerous enough as it is, and it's a bitter pill to swallow that one of the all-time F1 greats had survived a 21-year career in the fastest sport out there, but nearly met his demise while vacationing with his family.
